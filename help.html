How to Use the Extension

To predict the authenticity of an image from a webpage, simply secondary-click (right-click) on the desired image
and choose "Predict Image Authenticity"
from the Context Menu.

This action will trigger a pop-up displaying the "Manipulated Image Detector." The image will be promptly submitted to the detector, which will then provide a prediction regarding its authenticity.

Click here to open the "Manipulated Image Detector"
as a new tab of your browser.

How Does the Detector Work?

This Chrome extension serves as a companion to the "Manipulated Image Detector and Recommender"
Android application.

It utilizes an on-device model specifically designed to detect any potential manipulation or editing in an image.
This model is a custom convolutional neural network that underwent training on a subset of 3938 authentic images and 3938 manipulated images from the CASIA2 dataset.

The fully-trained model achieved an F1 score of approximately 87.3% when tested on a separate
subset of images from the CASIA2 dataset, which it had not been explicitly trained on.

Remember that all detections made by
the model are, in fact, predictions.
